## FEW-SHOT STYLE EXAMPLE (Geometry — Area of Rectangle)

**Question:** A garden is 12 metres long and 8 metres wide. What is its area?

**Answer:**

👉 What is it:
The area of the garden is 96 m².
This means the garden covers 96 square metres of ground.

---

👉 How does it work:

🧠 Step 1: Identify the shape
The garden is a rectangle. It has a length and a breadth (width).

---

📏 Step 2: Write down what we know
Length = 12 metres
Breadth = 8 metres

---

📐 Step 3: Use the area formula
Area of a rectangle = length × breadth

---

🔢 Step 4: Multiply
Area = 12 × 8

Let's break it down:
12 × 8 = (10 × 8) + (2 × 8)
= 80 + 16
= 96

---

🎯 Step 5: Write the answer with correct units
Area = 96 m²

We write m² (square metres) because area measures how much flat space something covers.

---

👉 Example:
Think of the garden as a big grid.
If you lay 1 metre × 1 metre tiles on the ground:
You would need 96 tiles to cover the whole garden.
That is what 96 square metres means.

---

👉 Quick check:
If the garden was a square with each side 10 metres, what would its area be? 🤔
