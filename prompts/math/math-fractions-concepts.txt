### TOPIC: Fraction Concepts — Understanding, Comparing, Multiplying, Dividing (Class 3–6)

This covers fraction UNDERSTANDING and comparison (Class 3–4), fraction multiplication and division (Class 5–6).
For fraction addition/subtraction with unlike denominators, see the separate fraction-add-sub topic.

---

#### Teaching approach by class level:

**Class 3 (Age 8–9): What is a fraction?**
- Start with SHARING: "You have 1 roti and 2 children. Each child gets 1/2."
- Top number (numerator) = how many pieces you HAVE.
- Bottom number (denominator) = how many EQUAL pieces the whole was cut into.
- "1/4 means: cut into 4 equal pieces, take 1 piece."
- Show on real objects: "Cut an apple into 4 pieces. Each piece is 1/4. Two pieces = 2/4 = 1/2."
- Equivalent fractions through pictures: "1/2 of a chapati = 2/4 of the same chapati. Same amount of food."

**Class 4 (Age 9–10): Comparing and equivalent fractions**
- Equivalent fractions: "Multiply top AND bottom by the same number. 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 = 4/8 = 5/10. They are all the SAME size."
- Simplifying: "Divide top AND bottom by the same number. 6/8 → divide both by 2 → 3/4."
- Comparing fractions with same denominator: "3/7 vs 5/7 — same size pieces, more pieces wins → 5/7 is bigger."
- Comparing with different denominators: make denominators same first, THEN compare tops.
- Mixed numbers: "1 and 3/4 means one whole plus 3/4 of another."
- Converting: mixed → improper: "1 3/4 = (1×4 + 3)/4 = 7/4." Improper → mixed: "7/4 = 1 remainder 3 = 1 3/4."

**Class 5 (Age 10–11): Fraction multiplication**
- Fraction × whole number: "3/4 × 2 means 3/4 taken 2 times = 6/4 = 1 1/2."
- Fraction × fraction: "1/2 × 1/3 means 1/2 OF 1/3." Multiply tops, multiply bottoms: (1×1)/(2×3) = 1/6.
- "OF means multiply in fraction language." "1/3 of 12 = 1/3 × 12 = 4."
- Cross-cancellation shortcut: "Before multiplying, if a top number and a bottom number share a factor, divide them first. Saves big numbers."
- Always simplify the final answer.

**Class 6 (Age 11–12): Fraction division**
- "Dividing by a fraction = multiplying by its FLIP (reciprocal)."
- "3/4 ÷ 1/2 = 3/4 × 2/1 = 6/4 = 1 1/2."
- WHY the flip works: "How many 1/2 pieces fit in 3/4? Think of cutting a 3/4 pizza into half-sized slices."
- Reciprocal: flip the fraction. Reciprocal of 2/3 is 3/2. Reciprocal of 5 is 1/5.
- Mixed number operations: convert to improper fraction FIRST, then multiply or divide.

---

#### Step format for fraction concept questions:

**For "what is this fraction / compare fractions" questions:**
1. **Draw it out in words**: "1/3 means one whole cut into 3 equal pieces, and we take 1."
2. **Show equivalent forms** if comparing: make denominators equal.
3. **Compare** the numerators.
4. **Real-life example**: rotis, pizzas, chocolate bars.

**For fraction multiplication:**
1. **Write both fractions** (convert mixed numbers to improper first).
2. **Multiply tops together** and **multiply bottoms together**.
3. **Simplify** the result. Convert improper to mixed if needed.
4. **Sense check**: "1/2 × 1/3 = 1/6. Is 1/6 smaller than both 1/2 and 1/3? Yes — because we took a PART of a PART."

**For fraction division:**
1. **Write the problem**.
2. **Flip the second fraction** (reciprocal).
3. **Change ÷ to ×**.
4. **Multiply** as above.
5. **Simplify**.
6. **Sense check**: "3/4 ÷ 1/2 = 1.5. How many halves fit in 3/4? One full half and half of another half. Yes — 1.5."

#### Common mistakes to prevent:
- "1/2 × 1/3 = 1/5" — NO. Multiply tops AND bottoms: 1/6. Children often ADD across instead of multiplying.
- When dividing fractions, flip the SECOND fraction, not the first.
- Fraction of a whole number: "1/4 of 20" is NOT "1/4 + 20." It is (1/4) × 20 = 5.
- Mixed number operations: ALWAYS convert to improper fraction first. "2 1/3 × 1/2" → "7/3 × 1/2 = 7/6 = 1 1/6."
